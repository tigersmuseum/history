Hampshire Regiment, 1/9th Battalion. Summary of Movements 1914-1919:

July 30th 1914Special Service Section called out for coast patrols.
Aug 5th 1914Battalion mobilised, sent to war stations on coast.
Aug 8th 1914Proceeded to Lincolnshire for patrol duties on the East Coast.
Oct 1914Returned to the South Coast for coast duty.
Nov 1915Converted into infantry battalion and mobilised at Chiseldon for service in East Africa, afterwards changed to Egypt, then France, and, finally, ordered to proceed to India.
Feb 4th 1916Embarked at Plymouth for India.
Feb 25th 1916Landed at Bombay and proceeded to Bangalore.
Dec 1916Proceeded to the North West Frontier for divisional training with the 16th (Indian) Division.
March 1917Proceeded to Simla Hills, Dagshai, and Jutogh.
April 1917Mobilised for Waziristan Expedition.
June 1917Proceeded to Ferozepore.
March 1918Proceeded to Muree Hills for three weeks, and then returned to Amballa.
Oct 1918Mobilised for Siberia to join Canadian Expeditionary Force.
Oct 29th 1918Left Bombay, called at Colombo, Singapore and Hong Kong.
Nov 28th 1918Landed at Vladivostock.
Dec 1918Left Vladivostock for Omsk (3,500 miles). Twenty three days in the train.
Jan 7th 1919Arrived at Omsk.
May 1919Proceeded to Ekaterinburg, Russia.
Aug 1919Returned to Omsk, and then to Vladivostock.
Nov 1st 1919Embarked Vladivostock for England, via Vancouver and Montreal.
Dec 5th 1919Landed Southampton.
Dec 5th 1919Demobilised.

Total Miles Travelled: About 52,000. Overseas: 3 years, 9 months
