Administrative Battalions

1st Administrative Battalion of Hampshire Rifle Volunteers, December 1860. Comprising: 1st, 8th, 11th, 13th, 15th, 16th, 18th, 24th, 25h Hampshire RVC. Headquarters at Winchester.

2nd Administrative Battalion of Hampshire Rifle Volunteers, 1860. Comprising: 4th, 5th, 6th, 23rd Hampshire RVC. Headquarters at Portsmouth. (London Gazette 22458, 11 December 1860)

Consolidated 1880 as 3rd Rifle Volunteer Corps

  • A, B, C, D, E: Portsmouth (5)*
  • F, G: Gosport (6)*
  • H: Havant (4)*
  • I: Petersfield (12)
  • K: Fareham (7)
  • L: Portchester (19)* foremerly Cosham

3rd Administrative Battalion of Hampshire Rifle Volunteers, December 1860. Comprising: 7th, 8th, 12th, 17th, 20th, 21st, 22nd Hampshire RVC. Headquarters at Fareham. Battalion broken up in 1868.

4th Administrative Battalion of Hampshire Rifle Volunteers, January 1865. Comprising: 2nd, 3rd, 10th, 14th, 19th Hampshire RVC. Headquarters intially at Lyndhurst, moving to Southampton in 1872.

Consolidated 1880 as 2nd Rifle Volunteer Corps

  • A, B, C, D: Southampton (2)
  • E: Lymington (3)
  • F: Christchurch (10)
  • G: Lyndhurst (14)
  • H, I: Bournemouth (19)

By 1883 included a company at Ringwood, which together withthe companies at Bournemouth, Christchurch and Lymington was detached in 1885 to form the 4th Hampshire Rifle Volunteer Company designated 2nd Volunteer Battalion Hampshire Regiment same year and a new company added 1900. To TF 1908 as 5th Battalion.

1st Administrative Battalion, Isle of Wight Rifle Volunteers, 1860. Comprising: 1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th, 5th, 6th, 7th, 8th Isle of Wight RVC. Headquarters at Newport.
